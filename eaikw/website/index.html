What's Everyday AI? (Glad You Asked)

Okay, so we're living through the biggest tech shift since the internet. Maybe bigger. And most of the AI content out there is...

  • 🤖 Way too technical — "Let me explain how transformers work for 90 minutes" (spoiler: most people just want to know if they'll have a job)
  • 🎪 Pure hype — "AGI will solve world hunger! Or kill us all! Or both!" (deep breath, everyone)
  • 📚 Painfully surface-level — "10 ChatGPT prompts that will change your life!" (narrator: they won't)

Everyday AI is different. We actually talk about real stuff:

  • ✓ How AI tools actually work in production environments
  • ✓ What AI means for jobs, education, and economic structures
  • ✓ Ethical implications: bias, displacement, power concentration
  • ✓ Practical demonstrations: "Here's how to build X with Y"
  • ✓ Industry perspectives: conversations with people doing the work

Led by Keith Williams

In collaboration with Fakhair and other AI practitioners, educators, and industry professionals.

We're building a community of people who want to understand AI deeply—not just use it, but think critically about its impact.

Here's The Problem

Companies have all the AI technology they could want. What they don't have? People who can actually implement it in the real world. There's this massive gap between "cool AI demo" and "production system that doesn't fall apart on Tuesday."

What We're Building

We're taking the solid foundations of Information Systems (databases, architecture, systems thinking) and adding modern AI on top. The result? Students who can design, build, and deploy smart systems that actually work—not just impress investors in a demo.

Graduates Will Be Prepared To:

  • ✓ Design, implement, and fine-tune machine learning and LLM models to solve business problems
  • ✓ Architect enterprise solutions that scale with AI capabilities
  • ✓ Apply responsible and ethical AI principles in system development and deployment
  • ✓ Lead teams through complex AI implementations
  • ✓ Communicate effectively with stakeholders at all levels
