The Journey

1982: Where It Started

I was 7 years old when I touched my first computer—a Tandy TRS-80. While other kids were playing outside, I was figuring out BASIC, fascinated by the idea that I could make machines do what I told them. That fascination never left.

43 years later, I'm still building. Still learning. Still fascinated.

The Education Path

I earned my BS in Information Systems because I wanted to understand how technology fits into businesses. Then I pursued an MS in Instructional Design (3.9 GPA) because I realized that knowing how to build things wasn't enough—I needed to know how to teach others to build.

That's 7 years of formal pedagogical training on top of 22+ years actually teaching at NJIT.

The Business Owner (23 Years)

I founded Webizly and ran it for 23 years. I learned what it means to build software that people pay for. I learned how to lead teams, manage clients, and deliver on time. I learned the difference between code that works in a demo and code that works in production.

The CTO Experience (4 Years)

As CTO of Anthiem Ventures for 4 years, I led engineering teams, built the Kabam Framework, managed portfolios, and worked with Fortune 500 clients. I learned how to scale systems, how to make architecture decisions that matter, and how to lead teams through complex technical challenges.

NJIT: 22+ Years (And Counting)

At New Jersey Institute of Technology, I've been teaching for 22+ years and now serve as Director of the Enterprise AI Bachelor's Degree program. I built the curriculum. I teach the courses. I see what students struggle with and what they need to succeed in the real world.

This isn't theory—it's preparing students for actual AI engineering roles.

Zambia: 2021-2022

I spent a year in Zambia training government engineers. I built hybrid courses, taught in 100°F heat, worked with limited infrastructure, and learned what it means to teach in environments where resources are scarce. If I can train engineers in Zambia, I can train your team anywhere.

Why No PhD?

People ask me this. Here's my answer:

"I don't want to write pointless grants. I believe in practice that builds theory, not the other way around. Computing is a practical science. I'm a builder, a creator, a magician, a sage, and an explorer. That's where I add value—not in academia's bureaucracy."

I chose to ship systems instead of publish papers. I chose to build businesses instead of chase grants. I chose students over citations. And I've never regretted it.

What I'm Building Now

I maintain an active AI research practice. I've built a 20,158-line production system demonstrating autonomous research, knowledge graphs, vector databases, multi-agent systems, and advanced RAG. This isn't academic research—it's production code with 100% test coverage.

I use this R&D to stay on the cutting edge while helping clients ship their AI systems.
